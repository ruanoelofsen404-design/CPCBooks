About the Book

A deeply personal testimony of grief, reflection, and hope.

Grief isn’t something you get over; it’s something you learn to live with. Written from personal, non-fictional lived experience, this book is an intimate look into walking through the valley of loss after losing a father. It stands as an honest testimony of choosing reflection, grace, and the hope of healing over silence. It is a vital resource for anyone navigating deep isolation, emotional pain, or questioning their faith in the middle of a storm.

About the Author

Ruan Oelofsen writes with courage, compassion, and lived truth.

Ruan Oelofsen is an author, Christian ambassador, and advocate for honest conversations around grief and physical disability. Living with Cerebral Palsy, Ruan writes from a place of deep resilience, using his story to break the silence around unresolved trauma, offer a voice to the voiceless, and demonstrate what it means to sit with people in their pain. He is dedicated to helping others find comfort, hope, and real grace.
